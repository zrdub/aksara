# Code of Conduct

## Our Pledge

We as members, contributors, and maintainers pledge to make participation in
AKSARA a harassment-free experience for everyone, regardless of age, body size,
visible or invisible disability, ethnicity, sex characteristics, gender identity
and expression, level of experience, education, socio-economic status,
nationality, personal appearance, race, caste, color, religion, or sexual
identity and orientation.

We pledge to act and interact in ways that contribute to an open, welcoming,
diverse, inclusive, and healthy community.

## Our Standards

Examples of behavior that contributes to a positive environment include:

- Demonstrating empathy and kindness toward other people.
- Being respectful of differing opinions, viewpoints, and experiences.
- Giving and gracefully accepting constructive feedback.
- Accepting responsibility and apologizing to those affected by mistakes.
- Focusing on what is best for the overall community.

Examples of unacceptable behavior include:

- Harassment, intimidation, stalking, or unwanted attention.
- Sexualized language or imagery, and sexual attention or advances.
- Trolling, insulting or derogatory comments, and personal or political attacks.
- Publishing others' private information without explicit permission.
- Other conduct that could reasonably be considered inappropriate in a
  professional setting.

## Enforcement Responsibilities

Maintainers are responsible for clarifying and enforcing standards of acceptable
behavior. Maintainers may remove, edit, or reject comments, commits, code,
issues, pull requests, and other contributions that are not aligned with this
Code of Conduct.

Maintainers who do not follow or enforce this Code of Conduct in good faith may
face temporary or permanent removal from project responsibilities by the other
maintainers.

## Reporting

Report Code of Conduct concerns through GitHub by contacting the repository
maintainers or, for behavior that violates GitHub's platform rules, by using
GitHub's abuse reporting tools. Reports should include the relevant links,
screenshots, timeline, and names of people involved when available.

Maintainers will handle reports as privately as the platform allows and will
only share information with people who need it to review and respond.

## Enforcement Guidelines

Maintainers will consider the impact, severity, and pattern of reported behavior
when deciding how to respond.

### Correction

Community Impact: Use of inappropriate language or other behavior deemed
unprofessional or unwelcome.

Consequence: A private written warning from maintainers explaining the violation
and requesting a change in behavior.

### Warning

Community Impact: A single incident or series of actions that harms the
community or discourages participation.

Consequence: A formal warning with instructions for future conduct. Continued
violations may lead to a temporary or permanent ban.

### Temporary Ban

Community Impact: A serious violation of community standards, including
sustained inappropriate behavior.

Consequence: Temporary loss of interaction with the project for a defined period.
Violating the ban may lead to a permanent ban.

### Permanent Ban

Community Impact: A pattern of violations, harassment, threats, or behavior that
makes the project unsafe for others.

Consequence: Permanent loss of interaction with the project.
